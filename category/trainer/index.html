Trainer

SK

MBA (Chicago), CFA, MSc (NUS), BCom (UWA)

Steven was previously an Investment Director with SoftBank China Venture Capital from 2013 to 2015. Based in Singapore, he was responsible for sourcing and evaluating private equity deals, financial structuring, fund raising and risk management for the firm. Steven joined SoftBank China Venture Capital from Chicago Aquarius Advisory where he was the Managing Consultant advising regional SMEs on financial structuring and corporate strategy.

Prior to that, Steven spent four years on the buy side managing equity portfolios, researching stocks, sectors and geographic regions. In addition, Steven has fifteen years of sell side experience, mainly as an equity analyst in the Asia Pacific market.

Steven was working with DBS Asset Management in Singapore as a Senior Portfolio Manager managing the Asia Pacific and Europe equity funds. Prior to this, Steven was working with UOB Asset Management in Singapore as a Portfolio Manager managing international funds with geographical responsibility for Europe as a strategist. Steven started his career in UOBAM as a senior investment analyst covering financials and real estate sectors for the Asia Pacific markets.

Prior to his buy side career, Steven was with Standard & Poor’s Asia Equity Research in the capacity of Director of Research cum Chief Asian Strategist from 2000 to 2005. He was personally responsible for the regional Telecoms, Media and Technology companies within Asia & Japan. He also pioneered the equity research for REITs in Singapore and Hong Kong for S&P in 2002. He had extensive management experience in his previous roles as Head of Research in J.M Sassoon & Co and PT BDNI Securities.

Steven is a Chartered Financial Analyst since 2001. He graduated with a Master of Business Administration with the University of Chicago in March 2005 and a Master of Science (Applied Finance) from the National University of Singapore in 2000. He also graduated with a Bachelor of Commerce from the University of Western Australia in 1991.

Nigel Foo CFA

BBA (NUS)

20 years capital market experience

Nigel has 20 years of experience in the financial markets spanning across multiple asset classes.

In the earlier part of his career, he was actively involved in the interest rate and foreign exchange markets while working for several sell-side banks including JP Morgan, Credit Suisse First Boston and HSBC. It was during his stint on the sell side where he honed his skills in research, trading and risk management across various financial instruments.

Since 2004, Nigel has been managing fixed income portfolios covering a full suite of products ranging from Global government bonds, Asian local currency bonds and Asian credits, both investment grade and high yield. He has strong experience managing index strategies for one of the largest global ETF firm State Street Global Advisors. Currently employed by First State investments, Nigel is well versed in various active fixed income strategies and was voted one of the most astute investor in Asian fixed income market by “The Asset” magazine in 2012.

George KOH

George has over 20 years of experience in both Investment Banks as well as Fund Management. Specialized in Banking & Financials, REITs and Property Developers. Notable sell-side stints at Paribas UK (ranked #7 European Banks for Euromoney), CLSA (ranked # 3 Asiamoney for Singapore Banks), Cazenove (Real Estate and Financials), ABN Securities (first independent analyst to cover Singapore REITs).

George has been covering Real Estate sector since the year 2000. He spent nine years on the sell side (Paribas UK, CLSA, ABN Amro Securities and Cazenove) covering European Banks, Asian Financials and Singapore Property market. George was the first foreign analyst to initiate coverage on the REIT sector in Singapore. Before that, George had over five years of Buy side experience, mainly as an equity analyst covering the Asia Pacific market with Schroders Investment Management and Morley Fund Management.

At Cazenove, George was the first foreign house analyst to initiate coverage on SC Global, Hotel Properties Ltd, OUE and CDL Hospitality REIT. He had managed the re-listing/placement on OUE post-acquisition by Lippo Group. He was also involved with the IPO of CapitaMall Asia and Rights issues by CapitaCommercial Trust, Ascendas-REIT, Fortune REIT & Frasers Commercial Trust.

He spent over five years on the buy side managing equity portfolios, researching stocks, sectors and geographic regions with stints at Schroders Investment Management (US$6bn Assets under management), Morley Fund Management (US$2bn AuM, renamed as Aviva Investors) and HP Wealth Mgt (US$250mil AuM).

In between Cazenove and HPWM, George served as a property consultant to foreign developer in Vietnam, with involvement in township development in Quang Ngai, and hospitality project in Da Nang.

George graduated with Bachelor of Business Administration (2nd Upper Honors) with the National University of Singapore in June 1996.

CKK

Kien Kah has over 20years experience in capital markets, specializing in Fixed Income. He started his career in Citibank Singapore in 1996 as a Treasury Management Associate and was subsequently posted to the G7 Money Market desk where he worked as a dealer for 4 years.

After a 2.5year stint in the Regional Treasury’s e-Commerce unit, Kien Kah was assigned to the Financial Institutional Sales Team, dealing in SGS and SGD Corporate Bonds and G7 Rates.

Kien Kah joined JP Morgan Singapore in 2005 as part of the Credit and Rates Sales Team covering South-East Asian Central Banks / Sovereign Wealth Funds and Financial Institutional clients.  Product coverage was focused on USD and EUR Rates and Credit – with a focus on the Asian Corporate Bond market.  He dealt in US and European asset-backed securities, US mortgage-backed securities, US Collateralized Mortgage Obligations, USD and EUR denominated corporate bond and all forms of credit derivatives (CDS, ITRAXX and CDX credit indices).  He made Executive Director in 2009 and experienced first-hand the Credit Crunch in 2007-8 and the European Debt Crisis during his 7.5year stint in JP Morgan.

More recently, Kien Kah joined MUFJ Securities in 2013, where he spearheaded MUFJ’s expansion into the non-JPY rates business.  His product focus was on USD and JPY rates, and repo.
